> **Prerequisite:** the Basic course. Advanced builds on every Basic limit — it does
> not replace them.

The **Advanced** certificate unlocks the higher-risk environments that Basic forbids —
**but only** with an **approved aircraft** *and* the **right permission**. The
certificate needs the written exam (**50 questions, 60 minutes, 80 %**) **plus** an
in-person **Flight Review**.

## What Advanced unlocks

- **Controlled airspace** (Class C, D, E) — with NAV CANADA authorization.
- **Within 3 NM of airports** and **1 NM of heliports** — following published
  procedures (Drone tool / CFS).
- **Operations near and over people.**

![Distance-from-people zones: &gt;30 m basic, 5–30 m and &lt;5 m over people require Advanced + an approved RPAS](/lessons/diagrams/people-distance-zones.svg)

- **5–30 m** from people (near) and **under 5 m / overhead** (over people) — each needs
  an **approved RPAS** for that environment.

## Safety Assurance (Standard 922)

Access to these environments depends on the **aircraft**, not just the pilot. Under
**Standard 922**, the manufacturer files a **Safety Assurance declaration** that the
RPAS meets the standard for a given environment:

- operations in **controlled airspace**;
- operations **near people** (5–30 m);
- operations **over people** (under 5 m).

<Caution>
**An approved aircraft is not a permission, and a permission is not an approved
aircraft — you usually need both.** Flying in controlled airspace requires a
safety-assured RPAS **and** NAV CANADA authorization.
</Caution>

## Beyond Part IX: the SFOC

Some operations fall outside Part IX entirely and need a **Special Flight Operation
Certificate** — e.g. **over 25 kg, BVLOS, more than 5 RPA at once, advertised events,
dangerous payloads, within 3 NM of a military aerodrome**, or above Part IX altitudes.

<Note>
The **Flight Review** is a practical, in-person assessment graded met/not-met across
nine items — see the Advanced *Operations & Flight Review* lesson.
</Note>

## Key takeaways

- Advanced = **controlled airspace · near airports/heliports · near & over people**.
- Each higher-risk environment needs a **Standard 922 safety-assured RPAS**.
- **Aircraft approval + authorization are separate** — you generally need both.
- Outside Part IX (e.g. BVLOS, >25 kg) → **SFOC**.

*Sources: RPAS 101 pp.18–22, 34–35, 65 · CAR Part IX Subpart 1 Div V · Standard 922 · TP‑15263 §1 (Advanced).*
