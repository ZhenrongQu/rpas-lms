Advanced operations often put you where aviation radio terminology is in use. Even when
you don't transmit, the **knowledge is essential** for situational awareness.

## ROC-A

- To **transmit** on aviation frequencies you need a **Restricted Operator Certificate –
  Aeronautical (ROC-A)**, obtained via the **RIC-21** study guide and an examiner.
- For Advanced pilots the **knowledge** is essential even if you only listen.

## Phonetic alphabet & key frequencies

- Use the **ITU phonetic alphabet** (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie…) for clarity.
- Default frequencies when none is charted:
  - **126.7** — uncontrolled airspace not near an aerodrome,
  - **123.2** — aerodromes with no specific frequency listed,
  - **121.5** — the **emergency** frequency, everywhere.
- **MF** (mandatory frequency, shown as "M" e.g. M118.5) and **ATF** areas have a stated
  frequency and radius in the CFS.

## Listening, broadcasting & emergencies

- Maintain a **listening watch** on the appropriate frequency even if you won't transmit.
- A **blind broadcast** states: who you're calling (a landmark/aerodrome), **who you are**
  (e.g. "DRONE123"), **what you're doing** (height AGL, location, duration), and **how to
  call you back** (callsign + frequency).
- **Do not call ATC on an aviation radio** unless pre-arranged — your ground antenna has
  poor range to a masked tower. For an emergency, contact ATC by **phone**; the **ACC
  shift manager** numbers are available 24/7.

<Note>
You can listen on aviation frequencies without a certificate, but you must hold a
**ROC-A to transmit**. RPAS have no assigned callsigns — invent a clear one like
"DRONE123".
</Note>

<Tip>
Memorise **121.5** (emergency), **126.7** and **123.2** — they come up constantly in
uncontrolled-airspace planning and on the exam.
</Tip>

## Key takeaways

- **ROC-A (via RIC-21) is required to transmit**; the knowledge is essential for Advanced.
- Defaults: **126.7 / 123.2 / 121.5**; know **MF** and **ATF** areas.
- Keep a **listening watch**; use a clear **blind-broadcast** format.
- Reach ATC by **phone** in an emergency, not the aviation radio.
