Operating in or near controlled airspace means understanding its structure and getting
permission **before** you fly.

## Airspace classes A–G

![Airspace ladder: Class A/B high-level, Class C/D/E controlled around airports to 3000 ft AAE, Class G uncontrolled where drones fly below 400 ft, Class F special use](/lessons/diagrams/airspace-classes.svg)

- **A / B** — high-level, IFR; not relevant to drones.
- **C / D / E** — **controlled**, typically around airports, surface up to ~**3,000 ft
  AAE** (a **control zone** extends upward from the surface).
- **F** — special use: **CYA** (advisory), **CYR** (restricted), **CYD** (danger).
- **G** — **uncontrolled**; most low-level airspace where drones operate (< 400 ft AGL).

## Operating in controlled airspace (C/D/E)

You must have **all three**:

1. an **Advanced** pilot certificate,
2. a **safety-assured RPAS** (Standard 922) for controlled airspace, and
3. **authorization from NAV CANADA**.

Request it through the **NAV Drone** app/portal. Submit **well in advance** (it can take
up to ~14 days, though often minutes to hours); the **closer to the airport, the longer
it takes**. Approval may come **with conditions** — comply with all of them.

<Caution>
Even with a NOTAM and an authorization, an RPAS pilot must **give way to all crewed
aircraft** — helicopters, planes, gliders, paragliders, balloons.
</Caution>

## Class F, GNSS & transponders

- **Class F CYR/CYD** — permission comes from the **controlling agency**, not NAV CANADA.
- **GNSS:** ≥ 4 satellites (7+ ideal); **DGPS** ≈ 10 cm, **RTK** ≈ 1 cm. High **solar
  activity (Kp ≥ 4)** and terrain **masking** degrade accuracy.
- **Transponders:** small RPAS don't carry them, so they need **prior authorization** for
  transponder airspace (e.g. Class C).

<Note>
A **control zone**, unless otherwise specified, extends from the **surface up to and
including 3,000 ft AAE**.
</Note>

## Key takeaways

- Know the ladder: **A/B high · C/D/E controlled · F special use · G uncontrolled**.
- Controlled airspace = **Advanced cert + Std 922 RPAS + NAV CANADA authorization**.
- Apply early via **NAV Drone**; obey every condition.
- **Always give way** to crewed aircraft, NOTAM or not.
