Advanced certification adds operating *types* beyond simple VLOS — and a practical
**Flight Review** you must pass in person.

## Extended VLOS (EVLOS)

Visual observers extend your effective range while the **see-and-avoid** function is
maintained by the crew. Plan for **clear comms**, defined **range limits**, **C2-link**
stress at distance, and **endurance** (don't fly to the battery's edge far from home).

## Sheltered operations

Operating **near or under structures** (e.g. bridges, buildings) reduces airspace
exposure but adds obstacles and **interference**.

- Set lost-link behaviour to **HOVER**, not RTH (RTH can climb into the structure).
- Confirm airspace authorization if the shelter sits in controlled airspace.

## Operations near and over people

These require a **risk assessment** and a **safety-assured (Std 922)** RPAS. Mitigate
with crowd control, briefed crew, alternate landing areas, and tight pre-flight checks.

## The Flight Review

An in-person, practical assessment graded **met / not-met** across **nine items**:

- describe the **site survey** process,
- describe **emergency** procedures (lost-link, fly-away, who to contact),
- describe how to **report** an incident/accident to Transport Canada,
- **pre-flight checks**, **take-off**, **navigate around obstacles**, **recognise
  distances**, and **landing**.

<Note>
Miss **any one** of the nine items and the review is unsuccessful — you re-take the full
review at least 24 hours later.
</Note>

<Tip>
After passing the Advanced written exam you're issued a **Basic** certificate — use it to
**practise your SOPs and skills before the review**. Don't let the review be your first
real run-through.
</Tip>

Currency still applies: complete a **recency** activity every **24 months**.

### Check your understanding

## Key takeaways

- **EVLOS** uses visual observers; **sheltered ops** favour **HOVER** over RTH.
- Near/over people → **risk assessment + Std 922 aircraft**.
- The **Flight Review** is nine practical items — **all** must be met.
- Practise SOPs on your interim Basic certificate first; keep **24-month** recency.
